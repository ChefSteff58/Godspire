# Economy sim — M12 baseline & Demeter redesign spec (2026-07-06)

Generated by `scripts/econ-sim.ts` (run `npx vite-node scripts/econ-sim.ts`), which models a run's
**gold sources** on the real core functions (`waveIncome`, `waveSpec` bounties, `demeterIncome`) so
the numbers ARE the game's numbers. The permanent guard lives in `tests/economy.test.ts`.

> **Reading the curves:** each column assumes the farm is *already at the listed tiers* at that
> wave — these are steady-state income **ceilings** per configuration, not an achievable-from-scratch
> build path. That's the right lens for finding the runaway, which is a ceiling problem.
> Bounty income is a lower bound (Hydra split-children aren't in the spec); farms dominate the late
> curve so it doesn't move the story.

## The diagnosis: farm income is unbounded and trivializes the economy

**Full 8-god board (every god main-maxed A5 + dip B2) costs 52,275 gold.** Here's when each
archetype has earned enough *cumulative* gold to have bought literally everything:

| Archetype | Affords the whole 52k board by |
|---|---|
| baseline (no farm) | **never** (≤w60) — healthy: you must choose what to buy |
| 1 Cornucopia A5/B2 | wave 48 |
| 1 Vault B5/A2 | wave 48 |
| 1 Vault + Golden Harvest (1.6×) | wave 40 |
| **4 Vault B5/A2** | **wave 26** |

**Per-wave income** (the tap): baseline runs 62 → 1,118/wave across w1–60 and never exceeds the
priciest single upgrade (4,200). Farms break it:

| wave | baseline | 1 Cornucopia | 1 Vault | 1 Vault +GH | 4 Vault |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1  | 62  | 707   | 175   | 243   | 514   |
| 20 | 391 | 1,131 | 1,036 | 1,423 | 2,971 |
| 30 | 413 | 1,203 | 1,338 | 1,893 | 4,113 |
| 40 | 765 | 1,605 | 1,970 | 2,693 | 5,585 |
| 50 | 677 | 1,567 | 2,162 | 3,053 | 6,617 |
| 60 | 1,118| 2,058| 2,883 | 3,942 | 8,178 |

A **4-Vault stack earns >4,200/wave (a top-tier T5 every single wave) from w31 on**, reaching
8,178/wave by w60. And it's not just stacking: because `foldUpgrades` **sums** every purchased tier
(not just the top one), a single maxed farm is already oversized —

- **Cornucopia A5/B2**: main A(1–5) folds `incomePerWaveAdd = 20+45+95+160+300 = 620`; the B2 dip adds only `+5×wave` → **base 20 + 620 flat + 5×wave ≈ 640+ gold/wave from turn one.**
- **Vault B5/A2**: main B(1–5) folds `incomeWaveScaleAdd = 2+3+4+7+12 = 28`; the A2 dip adds `+65` flat → **85 + 28×wave** (≈1,485/wave at w50).

So the runaway has three compounding causes: (1) tiers **sum**, (2) Vault scales **×wave** with no
ceiling, (3) farms **stack linearly** with no diminishing returns. `demeterIncomeMul` (4× cap) and
`incomeMul`/`bossBountyMul` (no cap) multiply on top.

## Redesign spec (S1) — rein in the source, per the locked decision

Goal: a single well-built farm is a *meaningful* income engine (comfortably beats no-farm) but can
**never alone bankroll the entire board**, and stacking farms hits hard diminishing returns unless
they're **clustered** — turning "spam farms" into a bounded spatial-optimization puzzle.

1. **Rescale Vault ×wave growth** down ~⅔: target a single maxed Vault ≈ **400–550/wave at w50**
   (vs 1,485). Concretely, cut `incomeWaveScaleAdd` tier values roughly 2→1 / 3→1 / 4→2 / 7→3 /
   12→4 (maxed 28 → ~11) and/or clamp the `×wave` term at a wave ceiling (e.g. `min(wave, 40)`).
2. **Per-farm soft diminishing returns.** The Nth farm on the field earns `income × DR^(N-1)` with
   `DR ≈ 0.72`, so a 4-stack ≈ 1 + 0.72 + 0.52 + 0.37 ≈ **2.6 effective farms**, not 4. (Rank a
   farm by a stable order — e.g. placement index — so the payout is deterministic.)
3. **Cluster synergy (the fun mechanic).** A farm within radius R of other farms earns
   `+8%/adjacent farm`, **capped at +30%**. This *partially* offsets the DR — so tightly clustering
   3–4 farms is the optimal play, but the cap means it can't undo DR past that. Net: rewards smart
   placement, stays bounded.
4. **Cap the multipliers the readers flagged:** clamp `incomeMul` (Pantheon) and `bossBountyMul`
   (Fate Bargain) to sane ceilings (e.g. 2.5× and 3×) so they can't compound the farm layer without
   limit.

**Target after S1** (to become the flipped-on guard assertions): no farm archetype exceeds the
priciest T5 (4,200) in per-wave income through w60; a 4-Vault stack earns < ~2× a single Vault
(DR working); a single maxed farm does NOT afford the full 52k board before ~w60.

## ✅ S1 shipped — after the redesign

Cornucopia flat 620→420 (20/40/70/110/180), Vault ×wave 28→10 (1/2/2/2/3), plus `stackMul` DR
(`1/(1+0.3(N−1))`) and capped cluster synergy (`+8%/neighbour`, ≤+30%, radius 120px). `bossBountyMul`
capped at 3×. Re-running the sim (`npx vite-node scripts/econ-sim-run.ts`):

| metric | before | after |
|---|---|---|
| 4-Vault stack per-wave @ w60 | 8,178 | 2,550 |
| 4-Vault first wave > a T5 (4,200)/wave | w31 | **never (≤60)** |
| 1 Vault affords the 52k board by | w48 | **never (≤60)** |
| 4-Vault stack vs 1 Vault (farm income @ w50) | 4.0× | **2.1×** (DR) |
| 1 Cornucopia affords the 52k board by | w48 | w57 |

A single well-built farm still ~doubles your income (rewarding); no single farm bankrolls the board;
stacking hits hard DR that clustering only partly (≤+30%) offsets — a bounded layout puzzle. All four
guard assertions in `tests/economy.test.ts` are green. Live-verified: 2 farms 40px apart at w30 paid
exactly `2 × floor(380 × 1.08 × 0.769) = 630`.
